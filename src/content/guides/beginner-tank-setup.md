## Your First Aquarium: Complete Setup Guide

Starting your first aquarium is easier when you make fewer decisions all at once. Focus on a stable tank, simple equipment, and fish that match your time and budget.

### Step 1: Choose a Tank Size You Can Maintain

For most first-time owners, a 10 to 30 gallon tank is the safest starting range. Smaller tanks change faster and punish mistakes more quickly.

### Step 2: Plan the Core Equipment

At minimum, most beginner setups need:

- a filter
- a heater if you are keeping tropical fish
- a basic LED light
- a water conditioner
- a test kit

### Step 3: Cycle Before Adding Fish

Never treat the first day as stocking day. Let the tank establish the nitrogen cycle before fish are introduced so ammonia spikes do not wipe out the setup.

### Step 4: Stock Lightly

Resist the urge to overfill the tank. A lighter stock level gives you more margin for learning maintenance, feeding, and compatibility.
